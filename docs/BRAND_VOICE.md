# SpendGuard Brand Voice

## Brand Voice Summary

SpendGuard speaks like a calm, trustworthy financial guardian.

It is direct, protective, and non-judgmental. It helps users make better spending decisions before money leaves their account. It does not shame users, lecture them, or overwhelm them with financial jargon.

SpendGuard’s job is not to make users feel bad about spending. Its job is to help them see the real tradeoff before they buy.

The voice should feel like:

> “I’m here to help you pause, think clearly, and protect the money that already has a job.”

Not:

> “You’re bad with money. Stop spending.”

## Core Personality

SpendGuard is:

- Calm
- Protective
- Honest
- Practical
- Non-judgmental
- Clear
- Slightly firm when needed
- Encouraging after responsible choices

SpendGuard is not:

- Cute
- Sarcastic
- Guilt-tripping
- Overly motivational
- Corporate
- Robotic
- Fear-based
- Too casual about serious money decisions

## Brand Archetype

SpendGuard is a **Guardian + Advisor**.

It protects users from risky decisions, but it does not control them. It gives a clear recommendation, explains the reason, and lets the user make the final choice.

The user should feel:

> “This app is on my side.”

Not:

> “This app is judging me.”

## Voice Principles

### 1. Protect without shaming

Bad:
“You can’t afford this. This is a bad decision.”

Better:
“This purchase may put pressure on money already reserved for bills.”

Best:
“Waiting is the safer move. This purchase would use money already set aside for upcoming commitments.”

### 2. Be clear, not dramatic

Bad:
“This purchase could destroy your budget.”

Better:
“This purchase would reduce your safe-to-spend amount too much.”

Best:
“This would leave only ₱850 safe to spend after bills and protected savings.”

### 3. Explain the tradeoff

Bad:
“Not recommended.”

Better:
“Not recommended because this affects your savings goal.”

Best:
“Not recommended. Buying this today would delay your laptop goal by around 2 months.”

### 4. Focus on timing, not morality

Bad:
“You should not buy this.”

Better:
“This may not be the right time.”

Best:
“This looks like a wait decision. The purchase may be safer after your next payday or after your bills clear.”

### 5. Make the math feel human

Bad:
“Risk score: 78. Liquidity impact: high.”

Better:
“This purchase takes a large part of your available money.”

Best:
“After protecting your bills, debt, emergency buffer, and goals, this purchase would use most of what is safely available.”

### 6. Give users a next step

Bad:
“Purchase denied.”

Better:
“Wait before buying.”

Best:
“Wait 48 hours, then check again after your phone bill is paid.”

## Tone by Situation

### Onboarding Tone

Warm, calm, and confidence-building.

Use:
“Let’s set up your spending guardrail.”
“Tell us what money is already spoken for.”
“We’ll protect this before calling anything spendable.”

Avoid:
“Complete your financial profile.”
“Enter all required information.”
“Submit your budget details.”

### Safe to Buy Verdict Tone

Confident and reassuring.

Example:
“Safe to buy. This purchase does not affect your bills, emergency buffer, or active goals.”

The user should feel permitted, not reckless.

### Buy with Caution Tone

Balanced and specific.

Example:
“Buy with caution. You can afford it, but it would leave less room until your next payday.”

The user should understand the risk without feeling scared.

### Wait Verdict Tone

Firm but supportive.

Example:
“Waiting is the safer move. This purchase would put pressure on money reserved for upcoming bills.”

The user should feel guided, not blocked.

### Not Recommended Tone

Clear, serious, and non-shaming.

Example:
“Not recommended right now. This would use money already reserved for debt, bills, or your emergency buffer.”

The user should feel protected, not embarrassed.

### Cooldown Tone

Supportive and discipline-building.

Example:
“This is a big purchase, so SpendGuard recommends a 48-hour pause. If it still feels worth it after the cooldown, check again with a clearer head.”

Avoid:
“You are impulse buying.”
“You need to control yourself.”

### Goal Delay Tone

Concrete and emotionally aware.

Example:
“Buying this today may delay your motorcycle goal by around 1 month.”

Better:
“This purchase is possible, but it comes with a tradeoff: your motorcycle goal may move back by around 1 month.”

### Debt Warning Tone

Careful and respectful.

Example:
“This purchase may use money needed for a debt payment due soon.”

Avoid:
“You have debt, so you should not buy this.”

### AI Explanation Tone

Plain-language coach.

Example:
“SpendGuard’s verdict comes from your numbers, not AI. The reason this is marked as ‘Wait’ is that your upcoming bills and emergency buffer leave less safe-to-spend money than the purchase requires.”

## Words SpendGuard Should Use

Use words like:

- safe-to-spend
- protected money
- money already spoken for
- upcoming commitments
- guardrail
- pause
- safer move
- tradeoff
- delay
- breathing room
- pressure
- check again
- clear head
- reserved
- protected savings

## Words SpendGuard Should Avoid

Avoid words like:

- broke
- poor
- irresponsible
- guilty
- failed
- denied
- rejected
- bad decision
- dumb purchase
- budget violation
- financial mistake
- overspending addict
- discipline problem

## Brand Copy Examples

### Welcome

Before you buy, ask SpendGuard.

Set up your real safe-to-spend number so you can check purchases before they become regrets.

### Onboarding

Let’s build your spending guardrail.

We’ll protect money for bills, debt, savings, and emergencies before calling anything safe to spend.

### Purchase Input

What are you thinking of buying?

Add the item, price, and why you want it. SpendGuard will check the numbers before you decide.

### Safe Verdict

Safe to buy.

This purchase does not affect your protected bills, emergency buffer, or savings goals.

### Caution Verdict

Buy with caution.

You can afford this, but it reduces your breathing room until your next payday.

### Wait Verdict

Waiting is the safer move.

This purchase would put pressure on money already reserved for upcoming commitments.

### Not Recommended Verdict

Not recommended right now.

This would use money that should stay protected for bills, debt, savings, or your emergency buffer.

### Cooldown

This looks like a pause decision.

SpendGuard recommends waiting 48 hours before buying. If it still feels worth it after the cooldown, check again.

### Goal Tradeoff

This purchase has a tradeoff.

Buying it today may delay your laptop goal by around 2 months.

### Empty State

No purchase checked yet.

When you’re about to buy something, ask SpendGuard first.

### Success State

Your spending guardrail is ready.

SpendGuard will now protect your bills, emergency buffer, debt payments, and savings goals before calculating what is safe to spend.

## One-Line Brand Rule

SpendGuard should always sound like:

> A calm financial guardian helping you pause before spending, without shame, fear, or confusion.
